# Warp DevRel 30-60-90 Day Implementation Plan

## Executive Summary

This plan outlines the phased rollout of Warp's 8 core Developer Relations programs over the first 90 days. The strategy prioritizes quick wins, community building, and sustainable program launches to maximize impact while managing resources effectively.

**Goal:** Launch all 8 programs with measurable traction and establish Warp as a leader in terminal developer relations.

| Phase | Days | Focus | Key Milestones |

| **Phase 2** | Days 31-60 | Expansion & Momentum | 6 programs live, 500+ community members |
| **Phase 3** | Days 61-90 | Scale & Optimize | All 8 programs running, 1,000+ community members |

# Key Risks & Mitigation

## Risk 1: Low Initial Participation
**Mitigation:**
- Invite existing Warp users directly
- Partner with coding communities
- Offer early bird incentives
- Leverage team networks

## Risk 2: Bounty/Grant Quality Issues
**Mitigation:**
- Clear submission guidelines
- Thorough review process
- Tiered approval system
- Community voting for validation

## Risk 3: Workshop No-Shows
**Mitigation:**
- Send multiple reminders
- Offer recordings for registrants
- Keep content high-quality
- Guest speakers for big draws

## Risk 4: Hackathon Coordination
**Mitigation:**
- Test all systems 1 week prior
- Have backup judges/mentors
- Clear communication plan
- Post-mortem after event

## Risk 5: Resource Constraints
**Mitigation:**
- Prioritize core programs
- Use automation where possible
- Recruit community volunteers
- Adjust timeline if needed
